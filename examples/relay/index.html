Lights Control With Tah using the Relay Shield

Tah is a Bluetooth Low Energy Open Source Development platform compatible with Arduino. Controlling Home appliances over bluetooth with Tah and Tah relay + sensor shield. Tah lets you control the Lights, Fan, TV, AC, Water Pump and many more devices.

Here we will show you how to control an electric bulb using a Tah and the Tah Relay shield.

Requirements

  • Tah
  • Tah Relay + Sensor Shield
  • Mains Socket
  • Electric Bulb
  • Screwdriver
  • Wire Stripper
  • 12V DC Adapter
  • Arduino IDE installed
  • iPhone 4S or newer with the Tah app installed

Step 1

To get started with this demo download the Tah Arduino Library and install it into your existing arduino IDE.

Download the Tah ArdSCL sketch and uplaod it to Tah.

Step 2

The Tah relay + sensor shield has two onboard relays to connect the electrical appliances to be controlled. The relays are connected to digital I/O pin 2 and pin 3 of the Tah.

Connect the Electric light bulb in series with relay 1 or relay 2.

Please take care that while connecting the electric bulb to the relays, no power is given to the Tah relay + sensor shield and that the main power is turned off.

Step 3

Once done setting up the electric connections, plugin the Tah board to the relay + sensor shield. Power up the Tah board and the main power supply.

Step 4

  • Open the Tah app on your iPhone
  • Scan for Bluetooth Devices
  • Connect with the device named TAH
  • Go to the IO Control

Step 5

Now get familier with Tah I/O control app and start controlling led and the electric bulb.

The Tah app gives you the access to control the Tah pins in Digital I/O, Analog and PWM mode. Tah has six analog channels and seven PWM channels. With PWM pins we can drive the Servo motor by changing the PWM slider shown in app, and vary the brightness of the led using analog slider.

Analog controls display the status of the analog channels.

To blink an onboard led at pin 13 using Tah IO controller by simply toggling D13 shown in apps.


Step 6

BRIGHTNESS CONTROL

Adjust the brightness of the led using Analog slider from 0 to 255. Either connect an external led to any of the PWM channels or simply see the changing brightness of the onboard led at pin 13.

Just change the pin mode to Analog and vary the slider and observe the change in the led's brightness.


Step 7

Now you can start toggling the digital I/O D2 or D3 as per relay connections to control the electric bulb.
